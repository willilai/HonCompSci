Information on Blue Whales

Size and Weight

Blue Whales are the largest animals to live in Earth's history. They can reach a size of 108 feet and on average weight about 100 tons. The size and weight of a blue whale can depend on where they are located. They are mainly located in three areas: Western and Eastern North Pacific, and Antartica. The Western North Pacific whales average to be around 92ft long and weigh on average 106 tons. The Eastern North Pacific whales average to be around 72.1ft and weigh on average 94.25 tons. The Antartic whales average to be around 77.1ft and wieght on average 121 tons.

Migration Pattern

Blue whales migrate around the world for two purposes: to breed, and to feed. Differnet groups migrate to different areas. In the summer, the Western North Pacific whales migrates to their feeding grounds off of the coasts of Alaska and Canada. In the winter, they migrate to low-latitude waters, including Hawaii. The Eastern North Pacific whales migrates annually to the U.S. West Coast in the summer and fall to feed. In the winter they migrate to their breeding grounds in the Gulf of California and the Eastern Tropical. The Antartic Whales migrate to the Southern Ocean in the summer, and then move into mid- and low-latitude habitats in fall and winter.

Food

A blue whale's diet entirely consists of krill except in certain areas.

A blue whale eats the kril by getting the krill to get into a group. Once the krill are gathered together, the blue whale opens its mouth as wide as it can go (about 90 degrees) to collect as much krill as possible. It swims through the pack, swallowing everything that is in the whale's path. A blue whale has a set of baleen, so the krill get stuck on the baleen allowing the whale to pump out the water and then eating the krill.
